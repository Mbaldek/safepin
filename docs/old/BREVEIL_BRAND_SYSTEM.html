01 — Origine

A name born from
two French verbs

Breveil is a coined word that fuses the courage to face the world with the tenderness of watching over those you love. It carries French DNA at its root and universal clarity in its sound.

Braver
French verb
To brave, to face with courage. The act of stepping out into the world despite uncertainty — and the strength to do so because someone has your back.
Veiller
French verb
To watch over, to keep vigil. The quiet, attentive care of someone who stays awake so others can feel safe. A guardian's promise, made silently.

03 — Typographie

The voice of
vigilant elegance

Cormorant Garamond carries the French heritage — elegant, high-contrast, refined without being precious. Plus Jakarta Sans brings warmth with subtly rounded terminals — distinctive enough to avoid generic tech-app feel, readable enough for incident alerts at 3am. Both are Google Fonts for maximum dev accessibility.

07 — Application

When to use what

Each element serves a distinct purpose across the product ecosystem.

Wordmark
Landing page header, app store listing, legal documents, press kit, partnerships. Always with generous clear space.
Monogram
App icon, favicon, social avatars, notification badge, loading state, splash screen. Recognizable at 16×16px.
Symbol
Map watermark, onboarding illustrations, community badge, merchandise, environmental graphics, motion identity.
08 — Règles

Brand guidelines

DO
  • Use the wordmark on Midnight or Ivoire backgrounds
  • Maintain minimum clear space of 1× the "B" height
  • Use the monogram for small contexts (under 48px)
  • Pair Cormorant Garamond display with Plus Jakarta Sans body
  • Use Veil (amber) for primary CTAs and highlights
  • Use Danger red exclusively for SOS and emergency states
  • Apply the symbol as a watermark at 10-15% opacity on maps
DON'T
  • Place the wordmark on busy photographic backgrounds
  • Stretch, rotate, or distort any brand element
  • Use Danger red for non-emergency UI elements
  • Mix the symbol with the monogram in the same lockup
  • Use Aurora as a background for body text (contrast)
  • Set the wordmark in lowercase or mixed case
  • Add drop shadows, bevels, or gradients to the logo
